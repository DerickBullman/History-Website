First Airliners

DC-3

DC-3, dominated the early airline industry with only 25 of the 300 planes up not being a DC-3, by the end of its production run in 1945, almost 13,000 would be produced, the plane would continue to fly due to its ability to take off and land on short, and unkempt runways.
Source

Lockheed Constellation

Lockheed constellation, The first Pressurized airliner which allowed it to go to more efficient altitudes. It would also be the first hydraulically boosted controls reducing pilot fatigue, which also allowed for longer trips. Orevill Wright, the first man to make powered flight, would also make his last in this aircraft, as a copilot during a test flight in Wright Field ohio. IT would be used around the world in agriculture, millitar, and freight roles.
Source

Gulfstream I

Gulfstream I, The first purpose built business class airplane, which further interlocked the world economy by allowing workers to easily cross large swaths of land and sea, first produced in 1959, it would create the entire section of planes that is business class. It would also be the first plane to have an auxiliary power unit, allowing for controls and other electronics to stay online in the event of an emergency.
