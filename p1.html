Early Planes

First Flight

Wright plane, the first powered and sustained flight aircraft in the world, first flown in 1903 it would get its patent in 1904, this would also end up being the U.S. Army's first plane bought for the price of $25,000. They would eventually make another $5,000 from it for surpassing specifications totaling around $900,000 in today's money. This plane intrigued the world and set the stage for other advancements in aviation.

Blériot XI

Blériot XI, one of the early monoplanes, was also the first place to cross the english channel. The success of a monoplane proved that even with the limited flight capabilities, monoplanes were capable of flight. He would also find success in airplane manufacturing for both the government, and in airshows.
